From the player for the players

First masterminded by ex-Liverpool player Craig Johnston, the Predators achieved worldwide acclaim while being worn by the likes of David Beckham, Zinedine Zidane and Alessandro Del Piero.

The original Adidas Predator was made of leather. It featured rubber ridges. It only came in a few colors, black with white stripes and red accents. This color scheme is now known as the main one for all predators. The first goal scored by a professional footballer using Predator boots in a top-level match was by John Collins for Celtic in April 1994. He scored the opening goal in a 1–1 draw at Ibrox Stadium against Rangers, direct from a free-kick on the edge of the penalty box

The Predator Accelerator version was released in 1998 to coincide with the FIFA world cup held in France. This boot featured a re-engineered TRX (traxion) outsole, asymmetrical lacing, fore-footed rubber vamps sewn into the leather, and a "feet you wear" insole. It has been regarded as one of the best boots Adidas have produced to date. The boot was modelled by some of the greatest players such as Alessandro Del Piero, Zinedine Zidane and David Beckham.

Adidas introduced PowerPulse in the Predator Pulse in late 2003, and continued with the Predator Absolute in November 2005. Adidas claims the PowerPulse sockliner shifts the boot’s centre of gravity closer to the point of impact, which they say offers more powerful shots on goal. Most of absolute's PowerPulse is 40g, however only Red version is 60g.

Inspired by the traditional Predator colorway of old, the Adidas Predator 18+ debut look is predominantly black with white 3 Stripes on the outstep and red and gold detailing on the inside collar and the sole plate, respectively. The inside is solid black with the trademark control elements being the only visible design feature.

  • David Beckham

    Clubs: Manchester United, Preston North End, Real Madrid, LA Galaxy, A.C. Milan, PSG

    It was white Predator Precision for Beckham the day that he scored the free-kick that put England into the 2002 World Cup. He certainly needed that level of precision to find the back of the net in such dramatic circumstances.

  • Zinedine Zidane

    Clubs: Cannes, Bordeaux, Juventus, Real Madrid

    Zinedine Zidane was wearing Predator Mania boots when he scored one of the most iconic goals in the history of the Champions League to win the 2002 final for Real Madrid against Bayer Leverkusen.

  • Xavi Hernandez

    Clubs: Barcelona, Al-Sadd

    Spanish legend Xavi wore adidas Predators from the very start of his career. By the time he was at the peak of his powers at the 2010 World Cup, the midfield metronome was wearing the Predator X on his feet, released earlier that year in time for the tournament in South Africa.

  • Steven Gerrard

    Clubs: Liverpool, LA Galaxy

    Predator Pulse boots played a part in a heroic chapter of Champions League history for Liverpool legend Steven Gerrard, wearing the traditional black colourway in Istanbul as the Reds claimed their fifth European title, as well as a red colourway earlier in that 2004/05 season.
